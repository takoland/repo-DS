Station specific regression model analysis

by Takako Omori | powered by

Data Info

We selected one of the busiest bike stations in San Francisco in order to achieve detail analysis. Utilizing machine learning, we predict the hourly usage of bikes in a day including arriving and leaving from one station. Place: Embarcadero BART Station (Beale St at Market St) Data amount: 37,919 usages for one year (2017-July to 2018-July)


Model: Kernel Ridge Regression

It learns the linear function from the data and create a prediction using the variables earned in exhaustive search utilizing GridSearchCV.


Analysis

  • RMSE: 26.98
  • R-squared: 0.91
  • The peak hour lasts extremely long with 12 hours where the number of bikers constantly exceeds 40 which start from 7 AM to 7 PM.
  • Similar number of usages are seen in the combination of time zone in the morning and the evening:
    • 6 AM & 9 PM – appx. 35 riders
    • 5 AM & 10 PM – appx. 25 riders
    • 4 AM & 11 PM – appx. 15 riders
  • The smallest number of riders is seen at 3 AM
  • The biggest number of riders is seen at 4 PM
  • Changes of the number of riders in the evening after the peak time zone is slightly slower than the changes in the morning before the peak time zone.
